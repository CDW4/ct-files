Classic Vegetable Soup
The clean base — sneaks in everything. Endless variations.
⏱ 30 min 📦 Batch 🧠 Brain
▼
Ingredients — Makes 6 servings
3 tbspExtra virgin olive oilalways EVOO, never canola
4 clovesGarlic, minced
1 largeOnion, diced
2 mediumSweet potatoes, cubed
2 cupsBroccoli florets
2 cupsCarrots, sliced
2 cupsZucchini, chopped
3 cupsSpinach or kaleadd last 5 min — wilts invisible
6 cupsBone brothalways bone broth, not regular
1 tspTurmeric
½ tspBlack pepperactivates turmeric — always pair
1Lemon, juicedfinish only — iron absorption
SaltSea salt to taste
Steps
1
Heat EVOO in large pot over medium. Add garlic and onion. Sauté 5 minutes until soft and fragrant — this is the anti-inflammatory base.
2
Add sweet potato, carrots, and broccoli. Stir to coat in the oil. Cook 3 minutes.
3
Pour in bone broth. Add turmeric, black pepper, and salt. Bring to a boil then reduce to simmer.
4
Simmer 15–20 minutes until sweet potato is fork tender.
5
Add zucchini and spinach/kale. Cook 5 more minutes. Spinach will wilt completely.
6
Squeeze lemon juice over the top. Taste and adjust salt. Serve warm.
💡 Tips
→Rotate vegetables every batch — beets, asparagus, celery, bell pepper all work
→Blend half the soup for a creamier texture she may prefer
→Add a can of white beans for extra protein without changing the flavor
→Keeps refrigerated 5 days, freezes 3 months
Curcumin from turmeric Collagen from bone broth Beta-carotene Iron + Vitamin C

Lentil Veggie Soup
The hero meal. Sneaks in everything. Batch Sunday, eat all week.
⏱ 35 min 📦 Batch 🧠 Brain ⭐ Hero
▼
Ingredients — Makes 8 servings
3 tbspExtra virgin olive oil
5 clovesGarlic, minced
1 largeOnion, diced
2 cupsRed or green lentils, rinsed
2 mediumSweet potatoes, cubed
2 cupsBroccoli florets
1 cupAsparagus, choppedUTI protective
3 cupsSpinachadd last — disappears
8 cupsBone broth
1½ tspTurmeric
1 tspCumin
½ tspBlack pepper
1Lemon, juiced
Optional1 can sardines, blended inB12 + DHA invisible
Steps
1
EVOO in large pot, medium heat. Sauté garlic and onion 5 minutes until golden.
2
Add sweet potato and broccoli. Stir 2 minutes.
3
Add rinsed lentils and bone broth. Stir in turmeric, cumin, black pepper, salt.
4
Bring to boil. Reduce to simmer. Cook 20–25 minutes until lentils are fully soft.
5
Add asparagus and spinach. Simmer 5 more minutes.
6
If adding sardines — blend them into a small cup of the broth first, then stir into pot. Completely undetectable.
7
Finish with lemon juice. Adjust salt. Serve warm with a drizzle of EVOO on top.
💡 Tips
→Red lentils dissolve into the soup — creamier. Green lentils hold shape — heartier.
→Iron from lentils + vitamin C from lemon = natural iron absorption boost
→This is the one to make every Sunday without fail
Folate from lentils Iron + Vitamin C UTI protection Curcumin Gut healing collagen

Fish Soup
UTI + brain + insulin in one bowl. Make this twice a week minimum.
⏱ 20 min 🧠 Brain 💊 UTI ⭐ 2x/Week
▼
Ingredients — Makes 4 servings
3 tbspExtra virgin olive oil
4 clovesGarlic, minced
1 mediumOnion, diced
1.5 lbsSalmon or cod, cut in chunkseither works, both excellent
1 bunchAsparagus, chopped 1-inchUTI protective — key ingredient
1 cupDiced tomatoes
5 cupsBone broth
1 tspTurmeric
½ tspBlack pepper
1Lemon, juiced + zested
FreshParsley, chopped to finish
Steps
1
EVOO in pot, medium heat. Sauté garlic and onion 4 minutes until soft.
2
Add tomatoes. Cook 2 minutes, stirring.
3
Pour in bone broth. Add turmeric, black pepper, lemon zest. Bring to gentle boil.
4
Add fish chunks and asparagus. Reduce to simmer. Cook 10–12 minutes until fish flakes easily.
5
Squeeze lemon juice in. Add parsley. Taste and adjust salt. Serve immediately.
💡 Tips
→Don't overcook the fish — 10–12 min is enough, it continues cooking in the hot broth
→Frozen salmon or cod works perfectly — thaw overnight in fridge
→Add spinach in last 3 min for extra brain greens
→Best made fresh — doesn't reheat as well as the lentil soup
DHA + EPA omega-3 UTI protection from asparagus Curcumin Collagen

Seafood Soup
Shrimp, fish, and vegetables in a rich golden broth. Caribbean-inspired.
⏱ 25 min 🧠 Brain 💊 UTI 🌍 Caribbean
▼
Ingredients — Makes 4 servings
3 tbspExtra virgin olive oil
5 clovesGarlic, minced
1Onion, diced
1Bell pepper, diced
½ lbShrimp, peeled and deveinedzinc from food naturally
½ lbCod or salmon chunks
1 cupAsparagus or okra, chopped
2Sweet potatoes, cubed
1 canDiced tomatoes
5 cupsBone broth
1 tspTurmeric
½ tspThyme
1Lemon, juiced
OptionalScotch bonnet pepper — tiny pieceauthentic Caribbean flavor, use sparingly
Steps
1
EVOO in large pot. Sauté garlic, onion, and bell pepper 5 minutes.
2
Add tomatoes and sweet potato. Cook 3 minutes stirring.
3
Pour in bone broth. Add turmeric, thyme, scotch bonnet if using. Simmer 10 minutes until sweet potato softens.
4
Add fish chunks and asparagus. Cook 5 minutes.
5
Add shrimp last — cook just 3 minutes until pink. Do not overcook shrimp.
6
Squeeze lemon, adjust salt. Serve immediately.
💡 Tips
→Shrimp goes in LAST — always. Overcooked shrimp is rubbery and unpleasant.
→Okra thickens the broth naturally — Caribbean tradition and good for digestion
→This is the most culturally resonant soup in the rotation for her background

Baked Garlic Herb Wings
She will always eat these. Start at shift start — ready by lunch. Never fry.
⏱ 40 min ⭐ Staple 📦 Easy
▼
Ingredients — Serves 3–4
2–3 lbsChicken wings, whole or split
3 tbspExtra virgin olive oil
1 tspGarlic powder
1 tspOnion powder
1 tspPaprika
1 tspDried thyme or Italian herbs
½ tspBlack pepper
1 tspSea salt
OptionalFresh garlic, minced — rub on wings
Steps
1
Preheat oven to 400°F. Pat wings completely dry with paper towel — this is key to crispiness.
2
In a large bowl, toss wings with EVOO and all seasonings. Coat every wing thoroughly.
3
Arrange on baking sheet in single layer — do not crowd. Use a rack over the pan if available for crispier skin.
4
Bake 35–40 minutes, flipping once at the 20-minute mark. Wings are done when golden and skin is crisp.
5
Rest 5 minutes before serving. Pair with roasted sweet potato and steamed or roasted broccoli.
💡 Tips
→Caretaker tip: put wings in oven at 9am — ready exactly at lunch with zero attention
→Sweet potato cubes can roast on same sheet at same temp — same pan, different area
→Make double batch — wings reheat well, use for dinner too with different sides
→Never sauce with store-bought sauces — they're loaded with sugar. EVOO + herbs is the formula.

Baked Salmon + Greens
15 minutes. Omega-3 powerhouse. Pair with whatever greens are on hand.
⏱ 15 min 🧠 Brain
▼
Ingredients — Serves 2
2 filletsSalmon (6–8 oz each)
3 tbspExtra virgin olive oil
3 clovesGarlic, minced
1Lemon, sliced + juiced
1 tspDried herbs (thyme, rosemary, or dill)
Salt + pepperTo season
4 cupsKale or spinachsautéed as side
1 bunchAsparagus (optional side)
1 cupBrown rice (cooked)if serving as full plate
Steps
1
Preheat oven to 375°F. Line baking sheet with parchment.
2
Place salmon skin-side down. Drizzle EVOO generously. Rub with garlic, herbs, salt, and pepper. Lay lemon slices on top.
3
Bake 12–15 minutes until salmon flakes easily with a fork. Do not overbake — it should still be slightly darker pink in center.
4
While salmon bakes — heat EVOO in pan. Sauté garlic 1 min, add kale or spinach. Cook 4–5 minutes until wilted. Season with lemon and salt.
5
Plate salmon over rice with greens and asparagus alongside. Squeeze fresh lemon over everything.
💡 Tips
→Frozen salmon works — thaw in fridge overnight or cold water 30 min
→The rice makes this a complete satisfying plate — she'll feel full and nourished
→Asparagus on the side adds UTI protection to a brain-health meal
DHA + EPA Vitamin K from kale UTI from asparagus Complete protein

Yellow Curry
Turmeric-rich, coconut milk base, Caribbean-familiar. Batch cooks beautifully — better day two.
⏱ 30 min 🧠 Brain 📦 Batch 🌍 Caribbean
▼
Ingredients — Makes 5 servings
3 tbspExtra virgin olive oil
5 clovesGarlic, minced
1 largeOnion, diced
1Bell pepper, diced
2 tbspYellow curry pasteMaesri or Thai Kitchen — check no added sugar
1.5 lbsChicken thighs, cubed — OR shrimp — OR salmon chunks
2 mediumSweet potatoes, cubedbest curry vegetable for her
2 cupsBroccoli florets
2 cupsSpinachadd last — wilts invisible
1 canFull fat coconut milknot light — the fat is the point
1 cupBone broth
1 tspTurmericadds to the curry paste benefit
½ tspBlack pepper
1Lemon, juiced
Serve overBrown rice or cauliflower rice
Steps
1
Heat EVOO in large pot medium heat. Sauté garlic, onion, and bell pepper 5 minutes until soft.
2
Add curry paste. Stir into the vegetables and cook 2 minutes — this blooms the spices and builds flavor.
3
Add sweet potato and broccoli. Stir to coat in curry base 2 minutes.
4
Pour in coconut milk and bone broth. Add turmeric and black pepper. Stir well. Bring to gentle boil.
5
Add protein — chicken thighs need 15 minutes, shrimp needs 3 minutes, salmon needs 8 minutes. Add accordingly.
6
Add spinach last 3 minutes before serving. Stir in lemon juice. Taste and adjust salt.
7
Serve over brown rice or cauliflower rice. Drizzle EVOO on top.
💡 Tips
→Tastes better day 2 and 3 as flavors deepen — perfect Sunday batch cook
→Full fat coconut milk only — do not substitute light coconut milk
→Check curry paste label for added sugar — Maesri and Thai Kitchen are clean
→Cauliflower rice instead of brown rice for lower insulin spike
→Caribbean and West African food traditions both have curry roots — culturally familiar for her
Curcumin from turmeric + curry Healthy fat from coconut milk Anti-inflammatory base Beta-carotene from sweet potato

Fish Plate with Greens & Rice
Pan-seared fish, garlic greens, brown rice. Clean complete meal. 20 minutes.
⏱ 20 min 🧠 Brain 💊 UTI
▼
Ingredients — Serves 2
2 filletsCod, tilapia, or salmon
3 tbspExtra virgin olive oil
4 clovesGarlic, minced
1Lemon
1 tspPaprika
4 cupsCollard greens or kale, chopped
1 cupBrown rice, cookedpre-cook Sunday batch
Salt + pepperTo taste
Steps
1
Season fish with paprika, salt, pepper, and a squeeze of lemon on both sides.
2
Heat EVOO in skillet over medium-high. Place fish presentation-side down. Cook 4 minutes without moving — this builds a golden crust.
3
Flip fish gently. Cook 3–4 more minutes until cooked through. Remove and rest.
4
In same pan, add remaining EVOO and garlic. Sauté 1 minute. Add collard greens or kale.
5
Add 2 tbsp water, cover, steam 5 minutes until greens are tender. Season with salt and lemon.
6
Plate: rice base, fish on top, greens alongside. Drizzle EVOO, squeeze fresh lemon.
💡 Tips
→Brown rice pre-cooked Sunday — just reheat with a splash of water or broth
→Collard greens are culturally familiar and deeply nutritious — first choice here
→Don't skip the lemon at the end — it brightens everything and helps iron absorption

Stuffed Bell Peppers
Colorful, complete, and she loves them. Bake and serve — simple.
⏱ 35 min 🧠 Brain 📦 Batch
▼
Ingredients — Serves 4
4 largeBell peppers, halved lengthwise, seeds removedmix colors if possible
1 lbGround turkey or lean ground beef
1 cupCooked quinoa or brown rice
3 clovesGarlic, minced
1 smallOnion, finely diced
2 cupsSpinach, roughly chopped
1 canDiced tomatoes, drained
2 tbspTomato paste
2 tbspExtra virgin olive oil
1 tspCumin
½ tspPaprika
Salt + pepperTo taste
Steps
1
Preheat oven to 375°F. Arrange halved peppers cut-side up in baking dish. Brush lightly with EVOO.
2
Heat EVOO in skillet. Sauté garlic and onion 3 minutes. Add ground turkey or beef. Cook until browned, breaking apart, 6–8 minutes.
3
Add spinach, tomatoes, tomato paste, quinoa, cumin, paprika, salt, pepper. Stir and cook 3 minutes until combined.
4
Spoon filling generously into each pepper half. Press down firmly to pack.
5
Cover dish with foil. Bake 25 minutes. Remove foil, bake 5 more minutes for slight char on top.
6
Rest 5 minutes. Drizzle EVOO and fresh herbs to finish.
💡 Tips
→Make a full tray — refrigerates 4 days, reheats in microwave in 2 minutes
→Colorful peppers are visually appealing — dementia patients respond to colorful plates
→Red and orange peppers are sweeter and more familiar than green
Complete protein Vitamin C from peppers Iron + C together Folate from spinach

How to get every nutrient in without her noticing.
