Attachment Expansion Strategy
The goal is not to replace her husband — it's to expand her circle of comfort so she feels safe with others too. This takes weeks of consistency, not days.
📸 SIBLING PHOTO PLAN
→Request photos from all siblings — group family chat
→Print 4x6 of each sibling, label name on back
→Create rotating display in her main living area
→Caretakers use photos as daily conversation starter
→Review new photos together as a weekly activity
→Sunday brunch reinforces these faces in real life
🎙️
Record His Voice
Short messages: "I love you, I'll be back soon." Play during distress. Bridges his absence without deception.
🤝
Consistent Safe Caretakers
Same 2 caretakers, same routine, every day. Repetition builds neural familiarity even in dementia.
💬
Therapeutic Fibbing
"He asked me to stay with you." "He called and loves you." Validated by dementia research — reduces distress compassionately.
🔄
Redirect Immediately
When she asks for him, redirect to a favorite activity within seconds. Replace the loop before it spirals.
👥
Agape Day Center
Structured exposure to new safe people daily. Single most powerful long-term tool for loosening the dependency.
🌸
Give Her Agency Daily
Let her choose: which snack, which music, which seat. Autonomy reduces anxiety and need to cling to one person.
⏰
Gradual, Never Forced
Never cold turkey. Gentle redirection, extended comfortable time with caretakers, slow expansion always.
📅 REALISTIC TIMELINE
Weeks 1–2: Same caretakers daily builds baseline familiarity
Weeks 3–4: Agape expands her safe social circle outside home
Month 2: New trusted faces reduce acute distress when husband is away
Month 3+: Comfort circle is wider — calm with multiple people
Always: Progress not linear. Regression happens. Celebrate small wins.
