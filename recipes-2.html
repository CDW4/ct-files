Chicken and Waffles
Southern comfort staple. Clean baked chicken, whole grain waffles, natural honey drizzle. She will always eat this.
⏱ 30 min 🌍 Southern ⭐ Breakfast Staple
▼
Ingredients — Serves 6
6 piecesChicken tenders or small thighs
1 tspGarlic powder
1 tspPaprika
½ tspOnion powder
½ tspBlack pepper
½ tspSea salt
2 tbspExtra virgin olive oil
3 cupsWhole grain or oat flour waffle mixBob's Red Mill or Kodiak Cakes — clean ingredients, higher protein
1Egg
¾ cupGoat milk or regular milk
2 tbspRaw honeydrizzle to serve — natural sweetener only
OptionalFresh berries on the side
2Brazil nuts on the sidedaily selenium — always alongside breakfast
Steps
1
Preheat oven to 400°F. Season chicken with garlic powder, paprika, onion powder, salt, pepper, and EVOO. Toss to coat.
2
Bake chicken on lined sheet 20–25 minutes until cooked through and golden. While chicken bakes, make waffles.
3
Mix waffle batter per package — egg, goat milk, mix. Stir until just combined, do not overmix.
4
Cook in waffle iron until golden and crisp. 3–4 minutes per waffle depending on iron.
5
Plate waffle with baked chicken on top or alongside. Drizzle raw honey over everything. Serve with fresh berries and 2 brazil nuts on the side.
💡 Tips
→Kodiak Cakes waffle mix — highest protein clean waffle mix available at Walmart, Target, and Sprouts
→Goat milk in the waffle batter adds nutrition and a subtle richness she already responds well to
→Raw honey only — never maple syrup with added sugar or artificial syrups
→Chicken can be prepped Sunday and reheated — waffles should always be made fresh
→Air fryer reheats leftover chicken perfectly — 5 min at 375°F restores crispness
Complete protein Selenium from brazil nuts Whole grain fiber from waffles Natural sweetener only

Classic Vegetable Soup
The clean base — sneaks in everything. Endless variations.
⏱ 30 min 📦 Batch 🧠 Brain
▼
Ingredients — Makes 6 servings
3 tbspExtra virgin olive oilalways EVOO, never canola
4 clovesGarlic, minced
1 largeOnion, diced
2 mediumSweet potatoes, cubed
2 cupsBroccoli florets
2 cupsCarrots, sliced
2 cupsZucchini, chopped
3 cupsSpinach or kaleadd last 5 min — wilts invisible
6 cupsBone brothalways bone broth, not regular
1 tspTurmeric
½ tspBlack pepperactivates turmeric — always pair
1Lemon, juicedfinish only — iron absorption
SaltSea salt to taste
Steps
1
Heat EVOO in large pot over medium. Add garlic and onion. Sauté 5 minutes until soft and fragrant — this is the anti-inflammatory base.
2
Add sweet potato, carrots, and broccoli. Stir to coat in the oil. Cook 3 minutes.
3
Pour in bone broth. Add turmeric, black pepper, and salt. Bring to a boil then reduce to simmer.
4
Simmer 15–20 minutes until sweet potato is fork tender.
5
Add zucchini and spinach/kale. Cook 5 more minutes. Spinach will wilt completely.
6
Squeeze lemon juice over the top. Taste and adjust salt. Serve warm.
💡 Tips
→Rotate vegetables every batch — beets, asparagus, celery, bell pepper all work
→Blend half the soup for a creamier texture she may prefer
→Add a can of white beans for extra protein without changing the flavor
→Keeps refrigerated 5 days, freezes 3 months

Lentil Veggie Soup
Deeply seasoned, built on chicken or vegetable stock. Cumin, coriander, smoked paprika — this tastes like a real soup, not a health project.
⏱ 35 min 🧠 Brain 📦 Batch ⭐ Staple
▼
Ingredients — Makes 8 servings
3 tbspExtra virgin olive oil
6 clovesGarlic, minceddo not reduce — garlic is doing flavor and prebiotic work simultaneously
1 largeOnion, diced
2Carrots, dicedadds natural sweetness and body — do not skip
3Celery stalks, dicedUTI supportive, diuretic, builds savory depth
1 canDiced tomatoesadds acidity and body — balances the earthiness of lentils
2 cupsRed lentils, rinsedred lentils dissolve into the soup — creamier result than green
2 mediumSweet potatoes, cubedblood sugar stabilizing, natural sweetness balances spices
2 cupsSpinach or kaleadd last — wilts invisible
1 cupAsparagus, choppedfolate and prebiotic — already in her seafood soup, shared benefit
8 cupsChicken stock or vegetable stockchicken stock gives deeper flavor — vegetable stock for fully plant-based version. Either works.
2 tspCuminprimary flavor driver — this is what makes it taste like a real soup
1½ tspSmoked paprikadepth and color — same role it plays in the jambalaya
1½ tspTurmericanti-inflammatory — invisible in the color of the soup
1 tspCorianderpairs directly with cumin — don't use one without the other
½ tspCinnamonsmall amount — rounds out the spice profile, undetectable as cinnamon
½ tspBlack pepper
SaltTo taste — season at the end
1½Lemons, juicedcritical finish — brightens the whole pot, boosts iron absorption
Fresh parsleyGarnish — optional but adds freshness
Steps
1
Heat EVOO in large pot over medium heat. Add garlic, onion, carrots, and celery. Sauté 7 minutes until soft and golden — this is the flavor foundation, don't rush it.
2
Add all dry spices — cumin, smoked paprika, turmeric, coriander, cinnamon, black pepper. Stir into the vegetables and cook 2 minutes blooming the spices in the oil. The pot should smell deeply aromatic at this point.
3
Add diced tomatoes. Stir and cook 3 minutes until tomatoes break down slightly into the spiced base.
4
Add rinsed red lentils and sweet potato cubes. Stir to coat in the spiced base.
5
Pour in chicken or vegetable stock. Stir well. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a steady simmer. Cook 20–25 minutes until lentils have dissolved completely and sweet potato is tender. Red lentils should disappear into the broth — that's correct.
6
Add asparagus and spinach or kale. Simmer 5 minutes until asparagus is just tender and greens are wilted.
7
Squeeze lemon juice over the entire pot. Taste and adjust salt now — lentils absorb a lot of salt so season properly at this stage, not earlier. Serve with EVOO drizzled on top and fresh parsley if using.
💡 Tips
→Blooming the spices in oil at step 2 is the difference between a flat soup and a deeply flavored one — do not skip this step
→Red lentils dissolve on purpose — they thicken the broth naturally into a creamy consistency without any blending
→Chicken stock gives noticeably deeper flavor than vegetable stock — use it unless cooking plant-based
→Lemon juice at the end is non-negotiable — it lifts the entire pot and directly boosts iron absorption from the lentils
→Tastes significantly better day 2 — batch cook Sunday and the flavor deepens all week
→If it gets too thick on day 2 — add a splash of stock or water when reheating
Folate Prebiotic fiber Plant protein Low glycemic Iron + B vitamins Anti-inflammatory spice base

Signature Seafood Soup
The family staple. Assorted seafood, Old Bay, creole seasoning, rich broth. Make it Sunday, eat all week.
⏱ 45 min 🧠 Brain 📦 Batch 💊 UTI ⭐ Staple
▼
Ingredients — Makes 6–8 servings
2 lbsAssorted seafoodany mix: white fish, salmon, shrimp, clams, crab, mussels
2 tbspExtra virgin olive oilmore as needed for sautéing
2 cupsCelery, dicednatural diuretic — UTI supportive
1–2 cupsOnion, diced
6 clovesGarlic, mincedprebiotic powerhouse — do not reduce
1 cupCarrots, diced
2 cupsPotatoes, dicedor swap sweet potato for lower insulin spike
1 cupZucchini, diced
½ cupLeeks, slicedwhite and light green parts only — prebiotic
½ cupAsparagus, chopped 1-inchUTI protective — keep in always
½ cupJerusalem artichoke, cubedstrongest prebiotic available — add gradually first time
1 cupPlum or crushed tomatoesoptional but recommended — lycopene + broth depth
2 quartsChicken stockthe staple base — keep as is
1 tbspOld Bay seasoning
½ tspCreole seasoningstart light — adjust to taste, reduce salt if adding more
1 tspSea salt
½ tspBlack pepper
1 tbspFresh thyme, finely chopped
1 tbspFresh Italian parsley, choppedgarnish only — add after serving
½Lemon, juicedfinish only — brightens everything
Steps
1
Heat EVOO in large pot over medium heat. Add garlic, leeks, onion, and celery. Sauté 5 minutes until soft and fragrant — this is the flavor foundation, don't rush it.
2
Add carrots, potatoes, and Jerusalem artichoke. Stir to coat in the oil. Cook 3 minutes.
3
Add crushed tomatoes if using. Stir and cook 2 minutes.
4
Pour in chicken stock. Add Old Bay, creole seasoning, thyme, salt, and black pepper. Stir well. Bring to a gentle boil.
5
Reduce to simmer. Cook 15 minutes until potatoes are nearly tender but not fully soft.
6
Add zucchini and asparagus. Simmer 5 minutes.
7
Seafood order matters — add in this sequence:
Clams and mussels first — 5–8 minutes until they open. Discard any that don't open.
Crab and firm fish next — 4–5 minutes.
White fish and salmon next — 4 minutes — they continue cooking in hot broth.
Shrimp absolutely last — 2–3 minutes only until just pink. Overcooked shrimp is rubbery.
8
Squeeze lemon juice over the finished soup. Taste and adjust seasoning — add more creole or Old Bay if needed.
9
Ladle into bowls. Garnish with fresh parsley. Drizzle EVOO on top of each bowl before serving.
💡 Tips
→Shrimp goes in LAST always — 2–3 minutes maximum or they become rubbery
→Jerusalem artichoke — introduce gradually first time, can cause gas during gut adjustment period
→Tastes better day 2 as flavors deepen — perfect Sunday batch cook
→Refrigerates 3 days — reheat on low-medium, never boil reheated seafood
→Keep chicken stock — it is the right base for this recipe, do not substitute
→Fresh parsley garnish adds vitamin C — boosts iron absorption from the seafood naturally
DHA + EPA from seafood Zinc from shrimp/crab Prebiotics from garlic/leeks/artichoke UTI protection from asparagus/celery Lycopene from tomatoes Vitamin C from parsley/lemon

Trinidad Callaloo
Trinidad style — dasheen leaves, coconut milk, okra, crab, scotch bonnet. Cultural comfort and brain health in one pot.
⏱ 40 min 🧠 Brain 🌍 Trinidad ⭐ Cultural Staple
▼
Ingredients — Serves 6
1 bundleDasheen leaves (taro leaves) or spinach as substitutedasheen available at Caribbean grocery stores in DFW — spinach works if unavailable
12–15Okra, slicedprebiotic fiber — thickens naturally
1 canFull fat coconut milk
1 cupWater or light chicken stock
½ lbBlue crab or crab meatzinc + B12 from food — authentic Trinidad callaloo essential
¼ lbSaltfish, soaked and flakedoptional but traditional — B12 from food
4 clovesGarlic, minced
1Onion, diced
4Pimento peppers, dicedshadow beni and pimento are the authentic flavor base
2 sprigsShadow beni (chadon beni / culantro)essential for authentic Trinidad flavor — Caribbean grocery
2 sprigsFresh thyme
1Scotch bonnet pepper, wholekeep whole for mild heat — pierce or remove for no heat
2 tbspExtra virgin olive oil or butter
SaltTo taste
Steps
1
Wash dasheen leaves thoroughly. Remove tough stems. Chop roughly. If using spinach — use 2 large bags.
2
In large pot heat EVOO over medium. Sauté garlic, onion, and pimento peppers 4 minutes.
3
Add dasheen leaves and okra first — stir to combine with the sautéed base. Then pour in the water or stock and coconut milk. Add shadow beni, thyme sprigs, and scotch bonnet whole. Stir well to bring everything together.
4
Bring to boil then reduce to simmer. Cook 20–25 minutes until dasheen is completely tender and okra has thickened the mixture.
5
Add crab and saltfish if using. Cook 10 more minutes.
6
Remove scotch bonnet and thyme sprigs. Use a swizzle stick or immersion blender to blend until smooth — traditional callaloo is fully smooth. Semi-smooth is acceptable if preferred. Blend thoroughlytency — traditional callaloo is swizzled smooth.
7
Taste and adjust salt. Serve over rice or with provisions — boiled yam, dasheen, sweet potato.
💡 Tips
→Shadow beni is non-negotiable for authentic Trinidad callaloo — find it at Caribbean or Latin grocery stores in DFW
→Keep scotch bonnet whole for subtle warmth without intense heat — remove before serving for zero heat
→Swizzle stick gives authentic texture — immersion blender works perfectly as substitute
→Freezes beautifully — make a large batch and freeze in portions
→Serve alongside rice and peas or macaroni pie for a complete cultural meal
Iron + Folate from dasheen/spinach Zinc + B12 from crab Prebiotic from okra Healthy fat from coconut milk Antioxidants from shadow beni

Jambalaya with Hidden Vegetables
Bold Louisiana flavor loaded with invisible nutrition. The seasoning masks everything — sneak in every vegetable you have.
⏱ 45 min 🧠 Brain 🌍 Louisiana/Caribbean 📦 Batch
▼
Ingredients — Serves 6
1 lbChicken thighs, cubed
½ lbShrimp, peeledzinc from food naturally
2 linksClean chicken andouille sausagesmall amount for flavor only — check no added sugar or nitrates
2 cupsBrown ricelower insulin spike than white rice
3 tbspExtra virgin olive oil
1Onion, dicedprebiotic
3Celery stalks, dicedUTI supportive diuretic
2Bell peppers, dicedholy trinity — vitamin C
5 clovesGarlic, minced
1 cupZucchini, finely dicedinvisible in jambalaya
1 cupCarrots, finely dicedinvisible when small diced
2 cupsSpinachstir in last — wilts completely invisible
1 canCrushed tomatoes
4.5 cupsChicken stocknot 3 cups — vegetables release extra moisture, this ratio prevents mushiness
2 cupsWatercombined with stock = 6.5 cups total liquid for 3 cups brown rice
½ tspCreole seasoningflavor depth only — not the primary seasoning. Reduce heat significantly.
1½ tspSmoked paprikaprimary seasoning — gives deep Louisiana color and smokiness with zero heat
1 tspGarlic powder
1 tspOnion powder
1 tspDried thyme
½ tspDried oreganoauthentic Creole flavor, zero heat
½ tspBlack pepperpresent but restrained
1 tspTurmericinvisible in the seasoning — anti-inflammatory sneak-in
2Bay leavesremove before serving — find both before plating
Steps
1
Heat EVOO in large heavy pot. Brown sausage slices 3 minutes, remove and set aside.
2
Season chicken with creole seasoning. Brown in same pot 5 minutes. Remove and set aside.
3
Sauté onion, celery, bell peppers, and garlic — the holy trinity — 5 minutes until soft.
4
Add carrots and zucchini — stir 2 minutes. These disappear into the dish completely.
5
Add crushed tomatoes. Stir and cook 2 minutes. Then add all dry seasonings — smoked paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, thyme, oregano, turmeric, black pepper, the small amount of creole seasoning, and bay leaves. Stir everything together until well combined. The smoked paprika is doing the heavy lifting here — the color and aroma should be deep and rich at this point.
6
Pour in the chicken stock and water — 6.5 cups total liquid for 3 cups rice. Add the brown rice and stir once to distribute evenly — do not stir again after this. Return the browned chicken and sausage to the pot. Bring to a full boil, then immediately reduce to a low simmer. Cover tightly and cook exactly 25 minutes — do not lift the lid at all. Every lift releases steam and adds condensation back, making rice mushy. At 25 minutes check — if rice is done but liquid pools, remove lid and cook uncovered 3–5 minutes to evaporate. Rest off heat lid off 5 minutes before serving — rice firms up during this window.
7
Uncover and lay shrimp on top of the rice — do not stir yet. Cover and cook 3 minutes only until shrimp turn pink. Overcooked shrimp is rubbery — watch the clock. Uncover, stir the shrimp in, then add spinach. Stir until spinach wilts completely, about 2 minutes.
8
Remove bay leaves — find them before serving. Taste and adjust salt and creole seasoning. Serve immediately while hot. Jambalaya continues cooking in its own heat — do not leave covered after serving.
💡 Tips
→Mushiness fix: 6.5 cups total liquid for 3 cups brown rice — vegetables release extra moisture. Do not exceed this.
→Lid rule: Cover and do not lift for 25 minutes. Every lift causes steam loss and mushy rice. Set a timer.
→Spice rule: Smoked paprika is the primary seasoning — creole is accent only at ½ tsp. This eliminates heat while keeping full flavor depth.
→Stir once after adding rice — then never stir again until shrimp step. Stirring releases starch and creates mushiness.
→Shrimp absolutely last — lay on top, 3 minutes only, watch the clock. Do not add earlier.
→Rest off heat 5 minutes lid off before serving — rice firms up significantly during this window.
→Finely dice all hidden vegetables — grated zucchini and carrots disappear completely.
→Tastes better day 2 — perfect Sunday batch cook. Reheat with a splash of water, medium heat, stir gently.
Complete protein Zinc from shrimp Vitamin C from bell peppers Curcumin from turmeric Prebiotics from holy trinity Iron from spinach

Curry Roti / Veggie Choka with Sada Roti
Trinidad street food made clean. Curry is turmeric-forward brain medicine. Choka is roasted vegetable pure comfort.
⏱ 45 min 🧠 Brain 🌍 Trinidad ⭐ Cultural Staple
▼
Curry Filling Ingredients — Serves 6
1 lbChicken thighs or chickpeas (channa)channa curry is the most traditional and nutritious option
3 tbspExtra virgin olive oil
3 tbspCaribbean curry powderChief or Turban brand — authentic Trinidad flavor
5 clovesGarlic, minced
1Onion, diced
2Pimento peppers, diced
2 sprigsShadow beni
1 sprigFresh thyme
1Scotch bonnet, whole
1 cupPotatoes or sweet potato, cubed
½ cupWater
SaltTo taste
Veggie Choka Option
2 largeTomatoes or eggplantroasted over open flame or oven broiled for authentic char
4 clovesGarlic, roasted with vegetable
1Onion, charred
2 tbspExtra virgin olive oil
Salt + pepperTo taste
Steps — Curry
1
Heat EVOO in heavy pot. Add curry powder and stir constantly 1–2 minutes — blooming the curry in oil is essential for deep flavor.
2
Add garlic, onion, pimento peppers, shadow beni, thyme. Sauté 4 minutes.
3
Add chicken or chickpeas and potato. Stir to coat completely in curry base.
4
Add water and scotch bonnet whole. Cover and simmer 20–25 minutes until tender and gravy thickens.
5

3
Serve alongside warm sada roti for dipping and wrapping.

Stewed Chicken
Trinidad stewed chicken — browning caramelized base, deep herb flavor, fall-off-the-bone tender. The ultimate comfort dinner.
⏱ 50 min 🧠 Brain 🌍 Trinidad ⭐ Cultural Staple
▼
Ingredients — Serves 6
3 lbsChicken pieces, skin onthighs and drumsticks give best flavor for stewing
2 tbspBrowning sauceGrace or Chief brand — essential for authentic color and flavor
2 tbspExtra virgin olive oil
1 tbspBrown sugar or coconut sugarsmall amount for caramelizing only — authentic technique
5 clovesGarlic, minced
1Onion, diced
4Pimento peppers, diced
2 sprigsShadow beni
2 sprigsFresh thyme
1Scotch bonnet, whole
2Carrots, dicedhidden vegetable — absorbs the stew flavor
1Sweet potato, cubedhidden vegetable — blood sugar stabilizing
2 cupsSpinachadd last — wilts invisible into the stew
1 cupWater or chicken stock
Salt + pepperTo taste
Steps
1
Season chicken with garlic, pimento, shadow beni, thyme, salt, pepper, and browning. Marinate minimum 30 minutes — overnight in fridge is better.
2
Heat EVOO in heavy pot over medium-high. Add sugar — let it caramelize and darken to deep amber 2–3 minutes. Watch carefully — this is the browning base. Do not burn.
3
Add marinated chicken to the caramelized sugar. Stir to coat every piece. Brown on all sides 8–10 minutes.
4
Add onion, remaining seasoning from marinade, carrots, sweet potato, scotch bonnet whole, and water or stock.
5
Cover and simmer on low 25–30 minutes until chicken is tender and falling off the bone. Gravy thickens naturally.
6
Add spinach last 3 minutes — stir in until wilted and invisible in the dark gravy.
7
Remove scotch bonnet and thyme sprigs. Taste and adjust. Serve over rice and peas or with provisions.
💡 Tips
→The caramelized sugar step is non-negotiable — it gives authentic Trinidad stewed chicken its color and depth
→Overnight marinade transforms this dish — plan ahead for Sunday batch cook
→Sweet potato and carrots absorb the gravy beautifully — she gets the nutrition without knowing
→Spinach in dark gravy is completely invisible — always add it
→Tastes significantly better day 2 — perfect Sunday batch

Stewed Pigeon Peas
Trinidad classic. Gungo peas in a rich herb and tomato base. One of the most nutritious legumes available — serve with rice or as a side.
⏱ 35 min 🧠 Brain 🌍 Trinidad 📦 Batch
▼
Ingredients — Serves 6
2 cansPigeon peas (gungo peas), rinsedor cook from dry — soak overnight, cook 45 min for deeper flavor
3 tbspExtra virgin olive oil
5 clovesGarlic, minced
1Onion, diced
3Pimento peppers, diced
2 sprigsShadow beni
2 sprigsFresh thyme
1Scotch bonnet, whole
1 canCrushed tomatoes
1 cupCoconut milkoptional but traditional and brain healthy
1 cupSpinachstir in last — invisible in dark stew
½ cupCarrots, finely dicedhidden vegetable
Salt + pepperTo taste
Steps
1
Heat EVOO in pot over medium. Sauté garlic, onion, pimento, shadow beni, and thyme 5 minutes.
2
Add carrots — stir 2 minutes. They will disappear into the stew.
3
Add crushed tomatoes. Cook 3 minutes stirring.
4
Add pigeon peas, coconut milk if using, scotch bonnet whole, salt and pepper. Stir well.
5
Simmer 20 minutes until flavors meld and stew thickens naturally.
6
Add spinach last 3 minutes. Remove scotch bonnet and thyme. Taste and adjust.
7
Serve over white or brown rice. Classic rice and peas combination.
💡 Tips
→Pigeon peas from dry have significantly deeper flavor — worth the extra prep time for Sunday batch cook
→Coconut milk adds brain healthy fat and authentic Caribbean richness — always include it
→Serve as rice and peas alongside stewed chicken for a complete authentic Trinidad plate
→Freezes perfectly — make large batch and freeze in portions
